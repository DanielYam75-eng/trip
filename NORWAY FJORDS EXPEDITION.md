# NORWAY OPTION 1 — FJORDS EXPEDITION (18 DAYS)
## Oslo → Hardangervidda → Fjords → Bergen

---

## OVERVIEW
This is a high-scenery bikepacking expedition through southern and western Norway, combining mountain plateaus, deep fjords, ferry crossings, and coastal valleys.

This route is significantly harder than Sweden:
- Very steep climbs
- Rapid weather changes
- Expensive food
- Frequent elevation gain/loss
- Some days require ferries or route adjustments

But it is also one of the most visually extreme cycling routes in Europe.

Daily distance: 40–90 km  
Difficulty: Medium → Very Hard  
Sleep: 30% wild camping, 40% cabins, 30% hostels  
Best for: strong riders with expedition mindset

---

## COST STRUCTURE (NORWAY IS EXPENSIVE)
- Food: €25–50/day (very expensive groceries)
- Accommodation: €40–120 when used
- Ferries: €5–25 per crossing
- Total trip estimate (18 days): €1400–€2500

---

## WEATHER SYSTEM (CRITICAL)
Norway weather is unstable:

- Day: 10–22°C (lower in mountains)
- Night: 2–12°C in high areas
- Rain: frequent and sudden
- Wind: strong in fjords and plateaus
- Key risk: hypothermia in wet + wind conditions

---

## TERRAIN SYSTEM
- Oslo region: rolling hills
- Hardangervidda: alpine plateau (most extreme section)
- Fjords: steep climbs + descents
- West coast: wetter, warmer, windy

---

# DAILY BREAKDOWN

---

## DAY 1 — OSLO ARRIVAL (0 km)
Arrive in Oslo. Assemble bike, check brakes and tires carefully. Stock food for 2 days. Sleep in hostel.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Very easy  
Cost: €80–140  
Notes: prepare for climbing-heavy route

---

## DAY 2 — OSLO → DRAMMEN (40 km)
Short warm-up ride west out of Oslo through suburban hills.

Sleep: Hostel or wild camp  
Difficulty: Easy  
Highlights: river valleys  
Notes: gentle intro before mountains

---

## DAY 3 — DRAMMEN → GEILO (TRAIN ASSIST OPTION)
Begin climbing toward mountain regions. Many riders take train part of this section.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Medium–Hard  
Highlights: mountain transition  
Notes: steep ascent begins

---

## DAY 4 — GEILO → HARDANGERVIDDA ENTRY (70 km)
Enter alpine plateau approach. Long steady climb.

Sleep: Wild camp  
Difficulty: Hard  
Highlights: alpine lakes  
Notes: wind exposure increases

---

## DAY 5 — HARDANGERVIDDA CROSSING (60 km)
One of the hardest days. High plateau riding.

Sleep: Wild camp  
Difficulty: Very Hard  
Risks: cold, wind, no shelter  
Notes: carry full food + emergency gear

---

## DAY 6 — HARDANGERVIDDA CORE (50 km)
Continued plateau crossing. Minimal civilization.

Sleep: Wild camp  
Difficulty: Very Hard  
Highlights: tundra landscapes  
Notes: survival-style day

---

## DAY 7 — DESCENT TO EIDFJORD (60 km)
Huge descent from mountains into fjords.

Sleep: Cabin or wild camp  
Difficulty: Medium–Hard  
Highlights: fjord reveal moment  
Notes: dramatic scenery shift

---

## DAY 8 — EIDFJORD → VOSS (70 km)
Climb again into fjord mountains.

Sleep: Hostel or cabin  
Difficulty: Hard  
Highlights: waterfalls, valleys  
Food: resupply in Voss

---

## DAY 9 — VOSS REST DAY
Recovery and resupply.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Easy  
Cost: €50–100  
Activities: bike maintenance, food prep

---

## DAY 10 — VOSS → FLÅM (60 km)
Steep scenic descent toward fjord villages.

Sleep: Hostel or wild camp  
Difficulty: Medium  
Highlights: fjord valley roads

---

## DAY 11 — FLÅM REST DAY
Fjord exploration day.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Easy  
Highlights: ferries, viewpoints  
Notes: tourism + recovery

---

## DAY 12 — FLÅM → SOGNEFJORD REGION (70 km)
Ferry + ride combination through fjord system.

Sleep: Wild camp or hostel  
Difficulty: Medium  
Highlights: water crossings  
Notes: ferry planning required

---

## DAY 13 — FJORD LOOP DAY (50 km)
Shorter technical day with ferry crossings.

Sleep: Cabin or wild camp  
Difficulty: Medium  
Highlights: coastal fjords  
Notes: logistics-heavy day

---

## DAY 14 — FJORD VILLAGES (60 km)
Ride through small settlements.

Sleep: Wild camp  
Difficulty: Medium  
Highlights: coastal scenery  
Notes: weather exposure

---

## DAY 15 — CLIMB TOWARD BERGEN (70 km)
Final major climbing section.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Hard  
Highlights: mountain-to-city transition

---

## DAY 16 — BERGEN ARRIVAL (50 km)
Final descent into Bergen.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Easy  
Highlights: city arrival + fjord views

---

## DAY 17 — BERGEN REST DAY
Recovery and logistics day.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Very easy  
Cost: €60–120  
Activities: sightseeing, bike packing

---

## DAY 18 — BUFFER / EXIT DAY
Prepare return travel.

Sleep: Hostel  
Difficulty: Very easy  
Notes: pack bike for transport

---

# BIKE RETURN SYSTEM (NORWAY)

1. Finish in Bergen (main endpoint)
2. Take train or flight to Oslo (bike allowed on train with reservation)
3. Most rentals require:
   - Return in Oslo OR
   - Approved Bergen drop-off point
4. Pack bike for airline transport if flying home
5. Ferry/train logistics may be required depending on rental agreement

---

# KEY CHALLENGES

- Hardangervidda crossing is extreme weather zone
- Norway food cost is very high
- Elevation gain is constant
- Weather can change hourly
- Wild camping is legal but must be weather-safe (wind exposure is critical)

---

# SUMMARY

Total distance: ~1000–1250 km  
Average per day: 55–75 km  
Difficulty: High expedition level  
Best feature: fjords + mountains + dramatic terrain shifts

This is a **cinematic-level cycling route** — one of the most beautiful but physically demanding options in Europe.
