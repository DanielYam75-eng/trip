# 🥇 Caucasus Crossing — #1 overall (perfect match)

Difficulty: extreme (10/10)  
Singletrack: strong (9/10)  
Views: outstanding (10/10)  
Resupply: moderate (6/10) – villages exist, but remote sections can reach ~4–5 days without proper stores  
Wild camping: exceptional (10/10)  
Swimming: very good (7/10) – rivers, alpine lakes, glacier streams  
Cost: very cheap (5/10)  
Crowds: low (3/10)  
Distance: ~1800–2200 km (varies by exact line)

Special about it:  
This is one of the most dramatic mountain regions on Earth, combining European + Asian scale wilderness, deep valleys, glaciers, and isolated villages.

Key sights:
- Ushguli (one of the highest inhabited villages in Europe)  
- Svaneti region towers and medieval stone villages  
- Kazbegi / Mount Kazbek views  
- Remote Caucasus passes above 2500–3000m  
- Deep river valleys perfect for swimming every day

# 🥈 Trans-Balkan Race Route — best wild Europe adventure

Difficulty: very high (9/10)  
Singletrack: strong (9/10)  
Views: excellent (9/10)  
Resupply: moderate (6/10) – small villages, sometimes 3–4 days remote  
Wild camping: excellent (9/10)  
Swimming: good (7/10) – rivers and mountain lakes  
Cost: cheap (6/10)  
Crowds: very low (3/10)  
Distance: ~1400–1700 km

Special about it:  
A raw traverse through Balkan mountains — a mix of forests, limestone peaks, shepherd trails, and forgotten villages.

Key sights:
- Durmitor National Park (Montenegro)  
- Tara River Canyon (one of deepest in Europe)  
- Prokletije mountains (“Accursed Mountains”)  
- Remote Albanian highlands  
- Karst plateaus with endless ridge riding

# 🥉 Trans Pyrenees — best balanced MTB expedition

Difficulty: very high (9/10)  
Singletrack: strong (9/10)  
Views: outstanding (10/10)  
Resupply: very good (9/10) – villages every 1–2 days  
Wild camping: very good (9/10)  
Swimming: very good (8/10) – rivers, lakes, mountain pools  
Cost: moderate (6/10)  
Crowds: medium (6/10)  
Distance: ~1000–1300 km

Special about it:  
A complete mountain wall between Spain and France — extremely varied terrain from alpine ridges to deep valleys and Basque forests.

Key sights:
- Ordesa y Monte Perdido National Park  
- Aigüestortes i Estany de Sant Maurici  
- Basque green mountains and cliffs  
- High ridge crossings along the main Pyrenean spine  
- Medieval mountain villages

## 4. Hope1000 (Swiss Alps Race Route)

Difficulty: extreme (10/10)  
Singletrack: elite (10/10)  
Views: outstanding (10/10)  
Resupply: very good (10/10) – villages/towns every 1–2 days  
Wild camping: moderate (7/10) – restricted in Switzerland, stealth required  
Swimming: very good (8/10) – alpine lakes and rivers, cold but frequent  
Cost: expensive (3/10)  
Crowds: medium (6/10)  
Distance: ~1000 km  

Special about it:  
One of the most intense MTB race routes in Europe, crossing the Swiss Alps with constant high passes, steep climbs, and world-class trail density.

Key sights:
- Swiss Alpine passes (Furka, Grimsel, Susten, etc.)  
- Glacier valleys and dramatic alpine walls  
- Suspension bridges over deep valleys  
- High-altitude lakes and ridgelines

# 7. Trans Dinarica Cycle Route — Balkan flow adventure

Difficulty: high (8/10)  
Singletrack: good (7/10)  
Views: excellent (9/10)  
Resupply: very good (8/10) – villages every 1–2 days  
Wild camping: excellent (9/10)  
Swimming: excellent (9/10) – rivers + Adriatic coast  
Cost: very cheap (7/10)  
Crowds: low (4/10)  
Distance: ~2000 km

Special about it:  
A huge traverse of the Dinaric Alps from Slovenia to Albania.

Key sights:
- Balkan karst limestone mountains  
- Adriatic coastal sections  
- Deep river valleys everywhere  
- Forested alpine ridges

 ## ❌ Eliminate: Trans Dinarica Cycle Route

### Why

MTB: moderate (7/10) – many gravel/forest roads, limited sustained singletrack  
Mountains: medium elevation, not true high-alpine terrain  
Adventure: good but softened by frequent villages and easy logistics  
Swimming: excellent, but no longer a differentiator at this stage  
Crowds: low-medium, but route is relatively accessible  

### Verdict
Great Balkan bikepacking route, but less technical and less extreme than remaining expedition-level routes

➡️ Removed

# 11. Tian Shan Traverse (Kyrgyzstan) — absolute expedition wilderness

Difficulty: extreme (10/10)  
Singletrack: strong (9/10)  
Views: outstanding (10/10)  
Resupply: very low (4/10) – remote sections can be 5–7+ days without stores  
Wild camping: exceptional (10/10)  
Swimming: moderate (5/10) – alpine rivers and cold lakes, but less frequent access  
Cost: very cheap (4/10)  
Crowds: extremely low (1/10)  
Distance: ~2000–2500 km

Special about it:  
One of the most remote and dramatic MTB expedition regions on Earth. Huge glacier valleys, high passes above 3500–4000m, nomadic yurts, and endless off-grid riding.

Key sights:
- Song-Kul Lake (high alpine nomad lake plateau)  
- Issyk-Kul Lake (massive mountain lake)  
- Terskey Alatau & Tian Shan glacier ranges  
- Remote yurt camps in alpine valleys  
- High passes like Tosor & Ak-Suu region
  
 ## ❌ Eliminate: Kyrgyzstan (Tian Shan + Celestial Divide)

### Why

Resupply: too extreme (3–4/10) – often 5–7+ days without shops  
Logistics: expedition-level (permits, borders, remote rescue)  
Swimming: weak (cold glacier rivers, few usable lakes for swimming)  
MTB flow: limited – long hike-a-bike + very rough terrain  
Trip fit: exceeds 3-week “self-supported adventure” comfort level  

### Verdict
World-class expedition destination, but too remote and logistically heavy for your MTB + swimming + frequent resupply goals

➡️ Removed
