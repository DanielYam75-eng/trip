# NORWAY OPTION 2 — MOUNTAINS / HARDANGER EXPEDITION (18 DAYS)
## Oslo → Rjukan → Hardangervidda → Western Mountains Loop

---

## OVERVIEW
This is the most physically demanding option in your entire set of routes.

Unlike the fjord-focused route, this version prioritizes:
- High mountain plateaus
- Long remote gravel sections
- Minimal towns
- Extreme weather exposure
- Continuous climbing + descending cycles

This is closer to a true expedition / survival-style bikepacking route.

---

## DIFFICULTY SUMMARY
- Terrain: Extreme
- Climbing: Constant (daily elevation gain is high)
- Weather: Unpredictable and harsh
- Services: Sparse
- Navigation: Medium difficulty (remote roads + gravel)

Daily distance: 40–90 km  
Difficulty: Hard → Extreme  
Sleep: 50% wild camping, 30% cabins, 20% hostels  
Best for: experienced endurance riders

---

## COST STRUCTURE (NORWAY HIGH COST)
- Food: €30–55/day
- Cabins: €60–140 when used
- Hostels: €50–120
- Total trip estimate: €1500–€2700

---

## WEATHER SYSTEM (VERY IMPORTANT)
Mountain Norway is dangerous if unprepared:

- Day: 8–20°C
- Night: -2 to 10°C in high zones
- Rain: frequent + sudden storms
- Wind: very strong on plateaus
- Risk: hypothermia even in summer storms

---

## TERRAIN STRUCTURE
- Oslo region: rolling hills
- Rjukan valley: steep industrial mountain entry
- Hardangervidda: Arctic plateau
- Western descent zones: fjords + deep valleys
- Final section: Bergen approach mountains

---

# BIKE RETURN SYSTEM (NORWAY)

1. End in Bergen (main endpoint)
2. Take train or flight to Oslo if required
3. Norwegian trains allow bikes (reservation required)
4. Rental bikes typically:
   - Return in Oslo OR
   - Approved Bergen drop-off point
5. Pack bike for flight if flying home

---

# KEY RISKS (IMPORTANT)

- Sudden weather shifts on plateau
- Wind chill even in summer
- Long distances between shelter
- Limited rescue access in Hardangervidda
- Physical fatigue accumulation

---

Average per day: 55–75 km  
Difficulty: EXTREME expedition level  
Best feature: maximum wilderness + mountain exposure
